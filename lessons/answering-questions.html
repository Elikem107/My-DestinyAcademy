English Lesson: Answering Questions

Answering Questions

Answering questions is an important skill in English. To respond correctly, you need to understand the type of question, focus on keywords, and structure your answer clearly. Below are the main question types with detailed explanations and examples.

1. Wh‑Questions

Wh‑questions start with Who, What, When, Where, Why, How. These questions ask for specific information. The answer must directly address what the question asks for.

  • Who: asks about a person or people.
  • What: asks about a thing, action, or idea.
  • When: asks about time.
  • Where: asks about a place.
  • Why: asks for a reason or explanation.
  • How: asks about a method, manner, or process.
Example:
Q: Who discovered penicillin?
A: Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin.

Tip: Focus on the wh-word to understand what information is required.

2. Yes/No Questions

Yes/No questions expect answers like Yes or No. You can also add extra information to make your answer complete.

Example:
Q: Did you complete your assignment?
A: Yes, I completed it yesterday.
A: No, I haven’t finished it yet.

Tip: Use full sentences to make your answer clear and polite.

3. Multiple Choice Questions

Multiple choice questions give you several options. Carefully read all choices and select the one that best answers the question.

  • Read the question carefully before looking at the options.
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first.
  • Choose the most accurate and complete answer.
Example:
Q: Which animal is a mammal? a) Crocodile b) Elephant c) Turtle
A: Elephant

4. Fill-in-the-Blank Questions

Fill-in-the-blank questions require you to complete a sentence with the correct word. Look at the surrounding words for clues such as tense, singular/plural, and meaning.

Example:
Q: The moon shines at ______.
A: night

Tip: Check for subject-verb agreement and context clues to find the right word.

5. Short Answer Questions

Short answer questions require concise answers. Give enough detail to be accurate, but don’t repeat unnecessary words from the question.

Example:
Q: What is the largest planet in our solar system?
A: Jupiter.

Tip: Use one or two sentences, keeping your answer complete and clear.

Important Keywords

  • options, correct, answer, eliminate context, clues, tense, agreement, concise, complete, accurate
