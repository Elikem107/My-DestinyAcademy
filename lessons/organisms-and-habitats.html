Organisms and Their Habitats

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what a habitat is and why it is important for organisms.
  • Identify different habitats and the organisms that live in them.
  • Explain how organisms adapt to their habitats.
  • Recognize the interdependence between organisms and their environment.

Lesson Content

A habitat is the natural home or environment where an organism lives. Every organism has specific needs for survival, and the habitat provides these needs: food, water, shelter, and space.

Types of Habitats

  • Forest: Home to trees, birds, insects, monkeys, and tigers.
  • Desert: Dry habitat for camels, lizards, snakes, and cacti.
  • Freshwater: Rivers and lakes with fish, frogs, turtles, and plants.
  • Marine: Oceans and seas with whales, sharks, coral, and seaweed.
  • Grassland: Open lands with lions, zebras, elephants, and grasses.
  • Urban: Areas with humans, pets, pigeons, and rats.

Adaptations

Organisms have special features called adaptations that help them survive in their habitats. Example: camels store water to survive desert conditions; fish have gills to breathe underwater.

Class Activities

  • List 5 organisms you see in your school compound and identify their habitats.
  • Draw a simple diagram showing 3 different habitats and label the organisms living there.
  • Discuss how a change in habitat (like deforestation) affects the organisms living there.

Assessment Questions

  1. Define habitat and explain its importance for living organisms.
  2. Give 3 examples of habitats and the organisms found in each.
  3. What is an adaptation? Give an example.
  4. Explain what might happen to organisms if their habitat is destroyed.
  5. Why do different organisms live in different habitats?
