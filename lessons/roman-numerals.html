Roman numerals

Introduction

Roman numerals are a system of writing numbers that originated in ancient Rome. They use combinations of letters from the Latin alphabet: I, V, X, L, C, D, and M. Each letter has a value, and by combining them, we can form any number.

Roman Numeral Values

  • I = 1
  • V = 5
  • X = 10
  • L = 50
  • C = 100
  • D = 500
  • M = 1000

Rules for Writing Roman Numerals

  • Letters are usually written from largest to smallest.
  • Do not repeat a symbol more than three times in a row.
  • When a smaller number appears before a larger one, subtract it (e.g., IV = 4).
  • When a smaller number appears after a larger one, add it (e.g., VI = 6).

Examples

  • II = 2
  • VIII = 8
  • IX = 9
  • XL = 40
  • LXX = 70
  • CX = 110
  • CM = 900
