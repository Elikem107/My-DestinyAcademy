Skip counting

Introduction

Skip counting is counting forward or backward by numbers other than one. It helps us to understand multiplication and recognize number patterns. For example, counting by 2s gives: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10... Counting by 5s gives: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25... Counting by 10s gives: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50...

Watch the Video

Key Points

  • Skip counting is a quicker way to count groups of numbers.
  • It helps us understand multiplication and repeated addition.
  • It is useful for counting money, time, and objects arranged in groups.
  • Skip counting patterns can be found in multiplication tables.
