Life Processes

Learning Objectives

  • Define what life processes are and why they are important.
  • Describe each life process in detail with examples.
  • Identify life processes in humans, animals, and plants.
  • Explain the role of life processes in survival and growth.

Lesson Content

Life processes are the vital activities that all living organisms perform to stay alive. Without these processes, organisms cannot grow, reproduce, or maintain a healthy body.

1. Nutrition

Nutrition is the process by which organisms take in food and use it for energy, growth, and repair. Humans and animals eat food like rice, meat, and vegetables. Plants produce their own food through photosynthesis using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide.

2. Respiration

Respiration is the process of releasing energy from food. It happens in all living organisms. Humans and animals breathe in oxygen which helps convert food into energy. Plants also respire, taking in oxygen at night.

3. Excretion

Excretion is the removal of waste products from the body. Humans excrete urine and sweat; animals excrete urine and feces. Plants excrete oxygen as a waste product of photosynthesis.

4. Growth

Growth refers to the increase in size and development of an organism over time. Babies grow into children, children into adults, and plants grow from seeds into mature plants.

5. Reproduction

Reproduction is the process of producing offspring to ensure the survival of a species. Humans and animals reproduce sexually, while plants can reproduce sexually (flowers) or asexually (cuttings).

6. Movement

Movement is the ability to move from one place to another or move body parts. Animals walk, swim, or fly, while plants may move parts like leaves or flowers in response to sunlight (phototropism).

7. Sensitivity (Response to Stimuli)

Sensitivity is the ability to detect and respond to changes in the environment. Humans react to hot or cold temperatures, loud sounds, and bright lights. Plants respond to sunlight and water direction.

Class Activities

  • Observe a plant in your home and list examples of growth, nutrition, and sensitivity.
  • Draw a diagram showing all seven life processes in humans.
  • Discuss in pairs how excretion helps maintain health.
  • Identify life processes in your pet or local animals.

Assessment Questions

  1. Define life processes and explain why they are important.
  2. Describe the process of nutrition in humans and plants with examples.
  3. Explain respiration and why organisms need it.
  4. List the seven life processes and give an example for each.
  5. Why is reproduction important for the survival of a species?
  6. Give an example of sensitivity in plants and animals.
