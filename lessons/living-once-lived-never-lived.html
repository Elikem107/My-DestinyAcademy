Living, Once lived and Never lived things

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the difference between living, once lived, and never lived things.
  • Identify examples of each category in daily life.
  • Explain the characteristics that define living things.

Lesson Content

All objects around us can be categorized as living, once lived, or never lived. Understanding this helps us learn about the environment and life around us.

1. Living Things

Living things are organisms that show all life processes: they grow, move, respire, reproduce, respond to stimuli, and excrete waste. Examples: humans, animals, birds, plants, insects.

2. Once Lived Things

Once lived things are objects that were once alive but are now dead. They no longer carry out life processes. Examples: wood from a tree, dead leaves, animal skeletons.

3. Never Lived Things

Never lived things are objects that have never had life. They never carry out life processes. Examples: rocks, water, soil, plastic, metals.

Assessment Questions

  1. Define living, once lived, and never lived things.
  2. Give 3 examples of living things and explain why they are living.
  3. Give 3 examples of once lived things and explain how you know they are no longer living.
  4. Give 3 examples of never lived things.
  5. Why is it important to know the difference between these three categories?
