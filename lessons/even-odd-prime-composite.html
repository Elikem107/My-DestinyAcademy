Math Lesson: Numbers

Keywords

composite, prime, even, odd, sieve, Erastothenes, classify

Even and Odd Numbers

Numbers can be classified as even or odd based on whether they are divisible by 2.

  • Even numbers: Numbers divisible by 2. Examples: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10...
  • Odd numbers: Numbers not divisible by 2. Examples: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9...

Example:

Check if 14 is even or odd:

14 ÷ 2 = 7 → divisible by 2 → 14 is an even number.

Prime and Composite Numbers

Numbers greater than 1 can be classified as prime or composite.

  • Prime numbers: Numbers with exactly two factors: 1 and itself. Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11...
  • Composite numbers: Numbers with more than two factors. Examples: 4, 6, 8, 9, 12...

Example:

Check if 15 is prime or composite:

Factors of 15: 1, 3, 5, 15 → more than two factors → 15 is composite.

Sieve of Eratosthenes

The Sieve of Eratosthenes is a method to find all prime numbers up to a certain limit. Steps:

  1. Write numbers from 2 up to your limit (e.g., 50).
  2. Start with 2, mark it as prime, then cross out all multiples of 2.
  3. Move to the next uncrossed number (3), mark it as prime, and cross out multiples of 3.
  4. Continue until all numbers are either marked prime or crossed out.

Quick Tips

  • 2 is the only even prime number.
  • 1 is neither prime nor composite.
  • Odd numbers can sometimes be prime, e.g., 3, 5, 7.

Exercise

  1. Classify the following numbers as even or odd: 7, 12, 19, 28.
  2. Classify the following numbers as prime or composite: 2, 9, 13, 20.
  3. List all prime numbers between 10 and 20.
  4. Find an even number that is also prime.
